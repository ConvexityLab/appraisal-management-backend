End-to-End AMC/VMC Processes & Procedures
Client onboarding & compliance setup
•	Execute MSA/SOW; load credit box, product menus, SLAs, fees, and escalation contacts.
•	Configure AIR/Dodd-Frank independence rules; add appraiser blocking rules and conflict checks.
•	Set invoice model (borrower-pay, client-bill, split), tax handling, and PCI/payment gateway.
•	Map delivery formats (PDF + MISMO XML; UCDP/EAD/EVO as needed).
•	Create ROV policy and template; define timeframes and evidence requirements.
•	Train client/broker users on order portal; set up API if using Encompass/Mercury/LOS.
Order intake & acknowledgment
•	Intake via portal/API/email; auto-acknowledge within SLA (e.g., 1–2 hrs).
•	Validate address/APN, property type, occupancy, transaction type (purchase/refi/SFR/DSCR), rush or special conditions.
•	Screen for PIW/ACE/waiver eligibility (if applicable) to avoid unnecessary orders.
•	Confirm due date, contact info, access instructions, and HOA/tenant constraints.
Product selection & risk triage
•	Determine appropriate product: 1004/URAR, Desktop/Hybrid, 1073 Condo, 1025 2–4, 1004D, BPO (ext/int), Evaluation, DVR/Field Review (2000/2000A), STR Feasibility, Rent comp (1007/216), Small-balance commercial when applicable.
•	Run pre-assignment risk screen: rural/complex, high-value, waterfront/view, new construction, condo litigation, comp scarcity, fraud red flags (rapid resale, related-party).
•	If risk high → require supporting add-ons (photos, inspection, permit report) or escalate product (e.g., from Desktop → Interior).
Quote, payment & borrower comms
•	If borrower-pay: send secure payment link; enforce collection before assignment unless client policy overrides.
•	If client-bill: confirm PO/fee schedule; note changes for change-in-scope.
•	Provide intro email/SMS to borrower with what to expect and timelines; capture any black-out dates for access.
Vendor panel management (standing SOP)
•	Credentialing: license verification, E&O coverage, background/disciplinary checks, W-9, ACH.
•	Geographic competency & product competency tagging (luxury view, rural, 2–4, condo, small-balance CRE).
•	Performance scorecards: turn time, revision/defect rate, CU/SSR risk, client CSAT.
•	Rotation + performance-based routing; avoid steering/reuse that violates AIR.
Assignment & engagement
•	Select 2–3 eligible vendors; confirm fee & ETA; assign to the best match per rules.
•	Issue engagement letter: scope of work, AIR independence, due date/time zone, required exhibits (photos, sketch, floor plan, permit docs), change-order policy, contact protocol, revision policy, late penalties (if any).
•	Track acceptance; auto-reassign if no response within SLA.
Inspection scheduling (when applicable)
•	Vendor (or property data collector) contacts borrower within 24 hrs to schedule; record attempts and scheduled date/time.
•	Log access constraints (gated, tenants, lockbox, pets); capture HOA/parking details for condos.
•	If desktop/hybrid: dispatch PDC/inspector with photo and geo-tag requirements.
Ancillary orders & data (as needed)
•	Permit/record report, HOA docs, plat/survey, flood cert, views/amenity photos, STR feasibility, rent comp addendum (1007/216), cost approach for new builds, environmental screen (SB9/ADU, wildfire, flood).
•	Track ancillary SLAs; attach artifacts to the order record; notify appraiser.
Work-in-process monitoring
•	Status cadence: Accepted → Scheduled → Inspected → Drafting → Submitted.
•	Watch turn-time and milestone SLAs; nudge vendors at T-24/T-48 to due date.
•	Handle scope changes (complexity, extra units, outbuildings) with client approval and fee/time resets.
Vendor submission (technical intake)
•	Require PDF + MISMO XML (if applicable), sketch, photos, location map, comps map, certifications, engagement letter compliance.
•	Auto-run ingestion checks: file completeness, XML validity, photo count/types, signature/date, license/E&O match, geotag (if required).
10) QC—multi-layer review
Level 1: Technical/UAD checks
•	Missing fields, UAD codes, market conditions addendum, comp distance/time, net/gross adjustments, cost/math consistency.
Level 2: Compliance/AIR
•	No prohibited contact or attempt at value influence; scope matches order; proper assumptions/extraordinary assumptions are disclosed; occupancy, intended use/users correct.
Level 3: Analytic & risk
•	Comp selection appropriateness (view/lot utility), bracketing of GLA/bed/bath, time adjustments support, concessions, market trend consistency, fraud signals (EXIF anomalies, photo reuse, listing copy pasted).
Level 4: Investor system checks (if applicable)
•	UCDP/SSR (GSE) and/or EAD (FHA) submissions; review CU/SSR warnings; resolve hard stops with appraiser; capture SSR findings in the file.
11) Client delivery
•	Deliver via client’s portal/API/email with the agreed package: PDF, XML, SSR/EAD feedback, QC memo (if used), and invoice (if client-bill).
•	Update LOS status (Encompass/Mercury/Valuelink) to Delivered/Complete; log delivery timestamp and recipients.
12) Revisions & ROVs (reconsideration of value)
•	Revision (defect/clarification): issue structured request referencing pages/lines; time-boxed turnaround (e.g., 24–48 hrs).
•	ROV: accept in policy format only (market-supported alt comps/data). Triage for merit, forward to appraiser without influence, track outcomes, and document rationale. Escalate to review appraiser when value change requested.
•	Maintain a full audit trail of all changes and communications.
13) Billing & payables
•	If borrower-pay: post payment; handle refunds/partials for cancelled orders per policy.
•	If client-bill: generate invoice; bundle monthly if contract requires; include SSR/EAD fees if pass-through.
•	Vendor pay: release on client acceptance or delivery + X days; ACH with remittance; 1099 at year-end.
14) Post-delivery tasks
•	1004D/Final: track completion/CO for “subject to” assignments; auto-remind client near project completion.
•	Field/Desk Reviews: trigger per variance thresholds, CU/SSR scores, or investor overlay.
•	Archiving & retention: store files securely per policy and state/federal requirements (e.g., 5–7 years).
Risk, Fraud & Quality Controls (embedded)
•	Identity/geo checks on inspection photos (EXIF/geo-hash), duplicate photo detection, date anomalies.
•	Non-arm’s-length screen: ownership chain, rapid resale, shared mailing addresses/LLC principals.
•	Market stress flags: rising MOI/DOM, price-cut rates, declining market note required.
•	STR legality & use: brief check where relevant (illegal use can impair value/marketability).
•	Permit/GLA deltas: reconcile MLS vs public record; require support or comment.
•	Vendor rotation & COI: prevent assignment to conflicted appraiser/agent; enforce cooling-off after complaints.
•	Escalation paths: complex rural/luxury → senior reviewer; fraud suspicion → compliance/legal.
Status codes & typical SLAs (example)
•	New/Received (0–2 hrs) → Assigned (same day) → Accepted (≤24 hrs) → Scheduled (≤48 hrs) → Inspected (≤5 days) → Drafting → Submitted → QC (24–48 hrs) → Delivered.
•	Rush tiers: 24–72 hr deliverables (with fee premium and vendor confirmation).
•	Attempt cadence: 3 contact attempts in 48–72 hrs before client alert.
Engagement letter essentials (template cues)
•	Scope (form, interior/exterior/desktop/hybrid), effective date, due date/time zone.
•	Required exhibits: sketch, floor plan/photos, market conditions addendum, rent schedule (if DSCR), STR note (if relevant).

•	Change-order policy and fee/time impacts; communication channel (portal only).
•	Security/PII handling and deliverables (PDF/XML/SSR/EAD).
Vendor performance management
•	Quarterly scorecards: turn time, revision rate, CU/SSR risk tier, defect rate, client CSAT.
•	Coaching for common defects; suspension for non-performance; recertification annually.
•	Geo/product expansion for high performers (luxury view/rural/2–4).
Data security & privacy
•	GLBA/PII controls; least-privilege access; audit logs; encryption at rest/in transit.
•	PCI scope for borrower payments; purge payment tokens after settlement.
•	Incident response and client notification protocol.
Nice-to-have automations (high ROI)

### Contruction Focus
1) Intake & Scope Lock-In
1.	Confirm engagement letter & product fit: purpose, client/intended users, use case (origination, secondary, portfolio, workout), report type (1004/70, 1025/72, 1073/465, 1004D, 1007/216, 1004C, desktop/hybrid), effective date(s), subject interest (fee simple/leased fee), and extraordinary/limiting conditions.
2.	Identify value type(s): as-is, prospective “as-repaired/as-completed,” prospective market rent, exposure/marketing time.
3.	Determine data expectations & comps scope: number/type of comps, distance/time brackets, condition/GLA/age bracket tests, rental comps (if applicable), income evidence (for tenant-occupied/STR/mixed-use).
4.	Collect “truth set” outside the report: deed chain, prior MLS, public record, plat, zoning map/code, FEMA flood, HOA docs, permit history, photos, AVM/iAVM, local market stats, rent surveys, cost references (e.g., MVS), and any client overlays.
2) Identity, Compliance, & Bias Guardrails
1.	Verify property identity: address/APN/geo, legal description, unit #s, and map pins match; no parcel or unit mix-ups.
2.	Licensing & competency: appraiser certified for property type/state; proper supervisory signatures; conflict-of-interest and prior service disclosures.
3.	USPAP/SOW: problem definition, scope reasonableness, credible approaches, appropriate assumptions (ordinary vs. extraordinary).
4.	ECOA/fair lending: neutral language, no prohibited factors; risk of inadvertent bias in neighborhood or condition commentary.
5.	Engagement vs. report alignment: client, intended use, value type, and effective date(s) consistent.
3) Contract & Interest Conveyed (purchase cases)
1.	Purchase contract completeness: fully executed by sellers/buyers, all addenda, concessions, personal property; arms-length test.
2.	Price reasonableness: does indicated value credibly “explain” contract price (not the other way around)?
3.	Concessions & financing terms: quantify impacts; ensure comps adjusted similarly or bracketed.
4) Neighborhood & Market Conditions
1.	Market narrative vs. evidence: supply/demand, DOM, concessions trend, price trajectory; do charts/data support words?
2.	Micro-location sanity: school tiers, adjacency influences (arterials, rail, commercial), view/noise, flood/wildfire; are these priced in?
3.	Competing submarkets: if comps cross boundaries, is it justified and consistently adjusted?
5) Site, Zoning, Highest & Best Use
1.	Site specifics: size, shape, topography, access, utilities, easements/encroachments; flood & environmental.
2.	Zoning & use compliance: conforming/legal nonconforming/illegal; ADU rules; STR ordinances (if relevant); density/FAR caps.
3.	Highest & Best Use tests (as-vacant / as-improved): legally permissible, physically possible, financially feasible, maximally productive—and tied to the indicated value scenario.
6) Improvements & Condition
1.	Building description: GLA, bed/bath count, basement/ADU, effective age, quality/condition rating consistency (vs. photos/MLS/prior).
2.	Health & safety / deferred maintenance: life-safety items, code issues; do they trigger as-is deductions or repair conditions?
3.	Renovations scope & recency: verify claims against permits, invoices, photos.
7) Approaches to Value (sanity + math)
Sales Comparison (always primary in residential appraisal)
•	Comp selection logic: proximity, time, bracket GLA/age/condition/lot/view; no sale “stretching” to fit target.
•	Adjustment support: paired sales, market extractions, contribution logic; realism check for condition, GLA ($/sf), lot, view, accessory structures.
•	Net/Gross % reasonability: within program/agency norms given property complexity; if high, is it explainable?
•	Reconciliation: weight aligns with comp quality/quantity/adjustment dispersion.
Cost (when relevant)
•	Replacement cost source, soft costs, entrepreneurial profit, site/indirects, externalities.
•	Depreciation support: age-life vs. market; effective age consistent with narrative.
•	Land value support: sales, allocation, abstraction—coherent with market.
•	Source noted for cost factors
Income (when relevant)
•	Market rent derivation: 1007 or survey consistent with subject utility, condition, micro-location; reconcile to leases. (Flag if appraiser used STR data instead of long term leases)
•	Vacancy/credit loss, expenses, reserves: market-supported.
•	Cap/GRM support: comparable income properties; reconcile overall rate to market/interest rate context.
8) Reconciliation & Reasonableness Tests
1.	Cross-approach triangulation: does cost/income corroborate sales (where applicable)?
2.	Sensitivity checks: small swings in key adjustments—does value remain stable?
3.	Time adjustment logic: are sale dates normalized to effective date with market-supported rates?
4.	Final value narrative: explains why chosen weight/comp(s) dominate; addresses outliers.
9) Photos, Maps, Exhibits, and Math Integrity
1.	Photo truth-test: do photos match ratings/adjustments; are views/adverse factors disclosed?
2.	Sketch vs. public record/MLS: GLA and room count consistency; basement/bonus/ADU treatment.
3.	Arithmetic and transpositions: grid math, $/sf consistency, rounding, unit of comparison alignment.
10) Red Flags & Fraud/Risk Prompts
•	Comp clustering from same seller/builder or common intermediaries.
•	Serial flips / rapid resales with large price jumps lacking renovation proof.
•	Non-arm’s-length signals; unusual concessions; undisclosed personal property.
•	“Perfect match” comps far away while good local comps ignored.
•	Photos reused or AI-altered; MLS mismatches; permit claims without records.
•	Zoning/STR legality glossed over; H&BU not reconciled with actual use.
•	Income overstated vs. market; expense omissions; cap rate too low.
•	AVM/iAVM dispersion vs. opinion > tolerance; valuation materially above comp pack without support.
11) Report Compliance & Deliverables
•	Required forms addenda (1004D, 1007/216, rent rolls, cost backup).
•	Extraordinary assumptions & hypothetical conditions clearly labeled and appropriate.
•	Certifications & limiting conditions complete; supervisory roles documented.

Scenario Branches & Variables
A) As-Is vs. As-Repaired (Fix-and-Flip)
As-Is Review
•	Identify all deferred items & life-safety issues; quantify market reaction (deductions or condition adjustment).
•	Confirm H&BU as-improved vs. as-is use; if current use is suboptimal, reflect external/functional obsolescence.
•	Verify that rent (if any) reflects “as-is” condition and tenancy terms.
As-Repaired / ARV Review
•	Scope validation: detailed bid(s), line-item scope, materials/finishes tier, contractor credentials, permits.
•	Cost reasonableness: reconcile bids to cost services (e.g., MVS) ± contingency (commonly 10–20% depending on scope/unknowns).
•	Feasibility & market fit: will the post-repair product match the bracketed ARV comps (quality/amenities/features)?
•	Time & carrying risk: prospective value effective date, market trend during construction window, absorption risk.
•	Extraordinary Assumptions: repairs completed per scope; if scope is vague, require tighter documentation or downgrade certainty.
•	ARV comp set: use renovated exemplars in same micro-market; bracket condition/quality/features; avoid mixing “lipstick” with “gut-renovated” without robust adjustments.
•	Spread test (for lenders/investors): ARV – (acquisition + rehab + soft + carry + margin). If value leaves razor-thin equity, call it out.
B) Ground-Up Construction (Prospective “As-Completed”)
•	Plans/specs set: final drawings, elevations, materials schedule; match to valuation model items (GLA, bed/bath, garage, porch).
•	Entitlements: zoning compliance, variances, subdivision map status, permits pulled, impact fees.
•	Budget & draws: hard/soft/land; builder overhead & profit; draw schedule aligned to % complete curves.
•	Land value method: sales/allocations consistent with submarket; residual sanity check with cost & sales.
•	Comp set: new builds or recent model-match resales; if using older resales, adjust for age/spec delta.
•	Market absorption: if multiple units/phases, consider bulk discounts, sell-out pace, carrying risk.
•	Effective dates: inspection vs. prospective as-completed; ensure clear labeling and hypotheticals.
•	Builder incentives: adjust comps if upgrades/incentives distort prices.
C) Mixed-Use (Residential + Commercial Component)
•	Legal use & zoning: parking ratios, signage, hours, non-conformities.
•	Valuation approach: whole-property vs. component methods; ensure rent/expense splits and cap rates matched to each use.
•	Commercial rent support: market leases, TI/LC, vacancy; cap rate evidence for the commercial slice.
•	Residential comparables: ensure noise/traffic externalities reflected; separate STR vs. long-term rent economics if relevant.
•	Reconciliation: avoid double-counting; confirm that combined value aligns with market for mixed-use assets in the area.
D) Mid-Construction / Rehab In-Progress (Please see attached Mid-Construction guidance)
•	Percent complete verification: photo evidence, inspector notes, lien waivers; relate to draws taken.
•	Remaining cost to complete: validate vs. budget; add contingency for unknowns.
•	Feasibility check: any scope creep, change orders, or code issues?
•	As-is vs. as-completed dual indications: make sure each has its own effective date and assumptions.
•	Health & safety during construction: if not habitable, reflect in marketability/time on market.
E) Complex Properties (unique/rural/waterfront/large sites/ADU/STR)
•	Data scarcity plan: widen search methodically (time, distance, inferior/superior comps), then adjust with explicit support.
•	Rights & encumbrances: riparian/littoral rights, dock permits, view corridors, conservation easements.
•	Externalities: shoreline erosion, seawalls, wildfire/defensible space, insurance availability/ cost shocks—quantify where possible.
•	ADU/STR legality & economics: confirm ordinances, permit status, occupancy limits, tax/fee regime; separate personal property.
•	Unit of comparison shifts: move beyond $/sf when amenity or site utility dominates (e.g., frontage, view tiers, dock length).

Quick Quant & Policy Guardrails (use as automatic checks)
•	Comp distance/time: justify >1 mile urban/>6 months with explicit market data; time-adjust if market moved.
•	Bracket tests: GLA, lot size, condition/quality, bed/bath, view—at least one superior & one inferior comp for each key attribute.
•	Adjustment sanity:
o	GLA: keep within demonstrated market $/sf range; avoid linearity if segments show diminishing returns.
o	Condition: ensure photos/remarks justify big condition deltas; don’t over-reward “cosmetic.”
o	Lot: use paired sales or lot extractions; beware over-monetizing yard size in dense infill.
•	Net/Gross: elevated percentages require narrative support or comp reselection.
•	Rent reasonableness: reconcile 1007 to private rent data/advertised listings; normalize for utilities, furnishings (STR), term.
•	Income math: apply realistic vacancy/credit loss; include reserves; cap rate sourced to market (not just survey medians).
•	Cost: include entrepreneurial profit & indirects; reconcile effective age vs. depreciation.
•	Exposure/marketing: match to market segment and condition (habitable vs. non-habitable).

### Additional Process Applicatble to Servicers
Additional items geared towards Servicers to protect asset value and cashflow through the full life of the loan, with special attention to early payment default (EPD) and performance drift.
Goals for Servicers
•	Prevent EPDs and curb loss severity
•	Keep collateral sound (condition/coverage/compliance)
•	Stabilize escrow (tax/insurance) and avoid payment shock
•	Detect fraud/occupancy misrep that elevates repurchase or loss risk
•	Prioritize interventions with clear, actionable alerts
Early Default & Performance Risk
•	EPD Propensity Model: Combines payment timing patterns (NSFs, partials, auto-pay drops), contract-to-value gaps, valuation disagreements, borrower profile signals, and macro shocks to score 30/60/90-day default risk.
•	Payment Friction Signals: Predict missed payments from escrow shortage risk, insurance premium hikes, HOA dues spikes, or utility liens; surface cure options (modest escrow spread vs immediate advance).
•	Refi/Prepay vs. Default Classifier: Predicts whether a rising-rate or falling-rate environment translates to churn or delinquency so they can deploy the right retention/collections strategy.
Collateral Surveillance (Condition & Value)
•	Aerial/Street CV Checks (quarterly): Roof aging, tarp/patches, exterior distress, illegal additions; widen value ranges and dispatch property preservation/inspection when thresholds breach.
•	Interior Quality Drift (when new photos appear): Detects downgrade vs origination condition; flags for re-inspection.
•	Automated Value Refresh + Liquidity: Monthly value band + P(sell in 90/180 days) to guide loss-mit and REO timelines.
Hazard, Disaster & Climate Triggers
•	Event-Driven Inspections: FEMA disaster footprints, wildfire perimeters, flood crest alerts → auto-order occupancy checks, loss-draft inspections, or BPOs with photo proof.
•	Insurance Cost Shock Model: Predicts premium/coverage changes in wildfire/coastal markets that cause escrow deficits → early borrower outreach and payment plan modeling.
•	Map Change Monitoring: FIRMs/flood zones, wildfire risk maps; re-compute insurance needs and LTV stress.
D) Escrow, Taxes & Insurance (ESI) Health
•	Escrow Shortage Forecaster: Month-ahead projection of escrow deficits; simulate monthly spread vs lump-sum cures.
•	Tax Delinquency Watch: Scrape/import county rolls; predict tax default probability and initiate advances or outreach.
•	Hazard Policy Lapse Detector: OCR/mail-room ingestion + carrier webhooks → real-time lapse alerts; auto-dialer/letter suite.
E) Occupancy, Tenancy & Income Integrity
•	Occupancy Change Risk: Non-invasive indicators (mail change events, rental listings in the area, STR activity flags, sudden utility usage shifts) → occupancy re-verification workflow.
•	DSCR Drift (Investor Loans): Rent AVM + vacancy/expense norms to monitor stressed DSCR; trigger cash-flow counseling or reserve use.
•	STR Legality Monitor: Ongoing scrape of local ordinances and license registries; flag illegal nightly rentals that jeopardize income or insurance.
F) HOA/Condo/Building Risk
•	HOA Financial & Litigation Parser (LLM): Extracts deficit trends, special assessments, litigation mentions from minutes/budgets; scores building risk.
•	Recertification/Inspection Milestones: Track 25/30/40-year structural recertifications and façade/Local Law equivalents; alert ahead of deadlines.
•	Super-Lien State Watch: HOA delinquency signals → proactive cure or loss-mit path in super-lien jurisdictions.
G) Title, Liens & Fraud During Servicing
•	Title Gap & Lien Surveillance: Monitor recordings, releases, assignments; detect silent seconds, mechanics’ liens, and new judgments.
•	Ring/Collusion Graph (post-origination): Ongoing graph analytics across parties (brokers, appraisers, buyers/sellers) to catch serial flips or settlement-agent anomalies tied to their portfolio ZIPs.
•	Assignment/Wholesale Flip Detector: Post-close transfer chains that imply equity stripping or artificial price lift.
H) Borrower Engagement Intelligence
•	Call Center NLP: Transcribe inbound calls; classify hardship signals (job loss, medical, rent issues), sentiment, and urgency to triage human outreach.
•	ROV/Dispute Triage (LLM): Classifies dispute strength, requests missing evidence, and drafts response; shortens cycle time and reduces escalation.
I) Portfolio-Level Controls & Playbooks
•	Servicer Watchlists: High-risk segments: disaster overlap + escrow shock + negative equity + EPD score → color-coded queues.
•	Counterfactual Stressing: Property-level shocks (−5/−10/−15% value, +20% insurance, +1% tax) → expected delinquency lift and advance needs.
•	REO Readiness Index: Combines condition risk + liquidity + legal hurdles to prioritize property preservation and broker engagement.
J) Governance, Privacy & Compliance for Servicing
•	Explainable Scores & Reasons: Every alert carries a clear reason code (e.g., ESI_SHORTAGE_RISK, OCCUPANCY_SHIFT, DISASTER_FOOTPRINT) and recommended action.
•	Bias/Fair-Use Guardrails: No protected-class features; periodic fairness audits on outreach and collections decisions.
•	Audit Pack: Time-stamped data lineage, model versions, and action logs—ready for investors and regulators.
Data & Integrations (practical)
•	Feeds: Tax/treasurer rolls, insurer notifications, FEMA/NOAA events, county recordings, HOA docs, imagery, hazard maps, MLS/media (licensed), servicing system events (payments, NSF, autopay), document imaging/OCR.
•	Systems: Bi-directional APIs with their core servicing platform, doc imaging, and call center.
Alerts & Actions (examples we can operationalize)
•	EPD>75 + EscrowShortage>Moderate → same-day counselor call + escrow workout letter.
•	DisasterFootprint=YES + RoofDamageScore>High → loss draft inspection within 72 hrs.
•	DSCR<1.0 (trend ↓) + STRIllegal=Possible → income re-verification + compliance letter.
•	TaxDelinquent=YES → initiate advance; borrower plan; confirm occupancy.
•	HOA_SpecialAssessment=High → payment shock modeling + proactive outreach.
Servicing KPIs to Track
•	EPD rate (30/60/90) ↓ | Roll rate ↓ | Cure rate ↑
•	Escrow shortage incidents ↓ | Policy lapse days ↓
•	Disaster-to-inspection SLA ↑ | Loss severity on default ↓
•	QC/Investor exceptions tied to valuation/fraud ↓
